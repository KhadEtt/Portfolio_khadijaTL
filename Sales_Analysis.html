Sales Analytics

In this project, I used the AdventureWorks database—a sample dataset provided by Microsoft that simulates the operations of a fictional bicycle company—to dive deep into sales analytics. The goal was to transform raw data into actionable insights by leveraging SQL for data extraction and cleaning, followed by Power BI for visualization. Through this analysis, I explored trends in product sales, identified top customer segments, and examined regional performance. In this article, I'll walk you through the steps I took to create a dynamic sales dashboard, share key findings, and provide recommendations that could drive strategic decision-making for any business.

Business Request & User Stories

The business requested an executive sales report for sales managers as the primary deliverable for this data analyst project. Based on this request, the following user stories were defined to guide the project and ensure that the acceptance criteria were consistently met throughout its execution.

Data Cleansing & Transformation (SQL)

To create the necessary data model for analysis and to address the business needs outlined in the user stories, the following tables were extracted and prepared using SQL.
One data source, sales budgets, was provided in Excel format and integrated into the data model during a later stage of the process.
Below are examples of SQL statements used for data cleansing and transformation to ensure the data was ready for analysis. 

            -- Cleansed DIM_Customers Table --
            SELECT 
              c.customerkey AS CustomerKey, 
              --      ,[GeographyKey]
              --      ,[CustomerAlternateKey]
              --      ,[Title]
              c.firstname AS [First Name], 
              --      ,[MiddleName]
              c.lastname AS [Last Name], 
              c.firstname + ' ' + lastname AS [Full Name], 
              -- Combined First and Last Name
              --      ,[NameStyle]
              --      ,[BirthDate]
              --      ,[MaritalStatus]
              --      ,[Suffix]
              CASE c.gender WHEN 'M' THEN 'Male' WHEN 'F' THEN 'Female' END AS Gender,
              --      ,[EmailAddress]
              --      ,[YearlyIncome]
              --      ,[TotalChildren]
              --      ,[NumberChildrenAtHome]
              --      ,[EnglishEducation]
              --      ,[SpanishEducation]
              --      ,[FrenchEducation]
              --      ,[EnglishOccupation]
              --      ,[SpanishOccupation]
              --      ,[FrenchOccupation]
              --      ,[HouseOwnerFlag]
              --      ,[NumberCarsOwned]
              --      ,[AddressLine1]
              --      ,[AddressLine2]
              --      ,[Phone]
              c.datefirstpurchase AS DateFirstPurchase, 
              --      ,[CommuteDistance]
              g.city AS [Customer City] -- Joined in Customer City from Geography Table
            FROM 
              [AdventureWorksDW2022].[dbo].[DimCustomer] as c
              LEFT JOIN dbo.dimgeography AS g ON g.geographykey = c.geographykey 
            ORDER BY 
              CustomerKey ASC -- Ordered List by CustomerKey

Sales Management Dashboard


The completed Sales Management Dashboard is designed with three pages to provide comprehensive insights. The Sales Overview page serves as a high-level dashboard, summarizing key metrics at a glance. The Customer Details page combines tables and visualizations to analyze sales trends by customer, while the Product Details page highlights sales performance over time, segmented by product. This structure ensures clarity and supports data-driven decision-making for sales managers.

Project Analysis

Analysis:

The dashboard includes three views: Sales Overview, Customer Details, and Product Details. Here's an analysis of each:

1. Sales Overview

  • Overall Sales Performance:
    • Total Sales: 16,351,550.34
    • Budget: 15,300,000
    • Sales Variance: 1,051,550.34, indicating that sales exceeded the budget by about 6.87%.
  • Sales by Product Category:
    • Bikes dominate with 93.93% of sales (15.36M).
    • Accessories and Clothing are much smaller portions, suggesting that focusing on bikes has driven most of the sales.
  • Top Customers and Products:
    • Top 10 Customers: Jordan Turner is the highest contributor, with 11,484 in sales.
    • Top 10 Products: The Mountain Bikes series is the best-selling product line, with sales figures close to or exceeding 900,000 each.
  • Monthly Sales vs. Budget: Sales and budget performance are relatively close throughout the year, with some peaks observed in April, June, and November.
  • Geographic Sales Distribution: Sales are spread globally, with high density in North America and Europe, indicating these as the key markets.

2. Customer Details

  • Sales Insights by Customer:
    • Similar overall sales figure as the Sales Overview (16,351,550.34).
    • Monthly breakdown of sales by each top customer, with fluctuations indicating varying customer spending habits.
    • Top Customers:
      • Jordan Turner, Maurice Shan, and Janet Munoz are leading customers, contributing significantly to yearly sales.
      • Monthly variations reflect possible seasonality or purchase patterns.
  • Sales by Region: Map visualization shows similar trends, with North America and Europe as primary markets.

3. Product Details

  • Sales by Product and Sub-Category:
    • Bikes are the main contributors, with sub-categories like Touring Bikes and Mountain Bikes leading sales.
    • Top Products by Sales:
      • Touring Bikes are the best-sellers with 50,381 units, followed by Mountain Bikes and Road Bikes.
      • Accessories (Helmets, Gloves) have lower sales, suggesting they are supplementary.
  • Monthly Sales by Product Category: Detailed breakdown shows Bikes with consistently high sales, while Clothing has smaller sales amounts monthly.

Key Takeaways:

  • Product Focus: Bikes drive primary sales, with specific models performing well.
  • Top Markets: North America and Europe are leading regions.
  • Top Customers: High-spending customers contribute significantly to sales.
  • Sales Performance: Actual sales exceeded the budget, with strong months in April, June, and November.

Recommendations:

  • Expand High-Performing Product Lines: Invest in expanding bikes and bundle accessories to increase sales.
  • Focus on High-Value Customers: Develop loyalty programs for top customers and use segmentation for targeted marketing.
  • Enhance Presence in Key Markets: Increase efforts in North America and Europe with local offers.
  • Boost Seasonal Sales: Implement targeted promotions in peak months and strategize for low-sales months.
  • Increase Budget for High-ROI Products: Review and adjust the budget, focusing on high-performing products.
  • Grow Accessories Segment: Research accessory demand and encourage accessory sales post-purchase.
  • Leverage Data Insights: Regularly review sales performance and gather customer feedback.
  • Develop E-commerce Strategy: Improve online shopping experience and offer exclusive online promotions.

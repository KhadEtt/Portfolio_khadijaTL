Marketing Analytics

In today’s competitive landscape, effective data visualization and analytics are crucial for understanding customer behavior and optimizing marketing strategies. Businesses face challenges such as declining conversion rates, reduced customer engagement, and inconsistent feedback scores, which directly impact performance and customer satisfaction.

This project aims to provide a comprehensive overview of key trends, analyze the underlying causes, and recommend actionable strategies. By examining conversion rates, customer engagement metrics, and sentiment analysis, we strive to identify areas for improvement and deliver targeted insights to enhance outcomes.

Problem Statement

  • Decreased Conversion Rates: Conversion rates fluctuated throughout the year, with significant drops during certain months, impacting overall revenue and growth potential.
  • Reduced Customer Engagement: A decline in social media views and interactions, particularly in the latter half of the year, signals a need for more engaging and effective content strategies.
  • Customer Feedback Challenges: While overall ratings remain stable, they fall short of the target, with mixed and negative feedback highlighting specific areas requiring attention.

Goals

  • Increase Conversion Rates: Identify trends and factors influencing low conversion months and recommend targeted interventions to optimize the customer funnel.
  • Enhance Customer Engagement: Analyze content performance to understand which formats and platforms drive higher interaction and use this insight to revitalize content strategies.
  • Improve Customer Feedback Scores: Leverage sentiment analysis and feedback patterns to address recurring issues, aiming to boost overall satisfaction and ratings.

By addressing these challenges, this project seeks to transform data-driven insights into actionable recommendations for measurable business improvements.

Data Model

The data model integrates customer, product, and engagement data to provide a comprehensive view of marketing performance and customer behavior. It includes both fact and dimension tables linked together for analytics:

Fact Tables:

  • Fact Engagement Data: Tracks campaign performance metrics like clicks, views, and engagement data.
  • Fact Customer Journey: Captures customer interactions across stages, with details like actions, duration, and visit dates.
  • Fact Customer Reviews: Contains customer ratings, reviews, and associated sentiments for products.

Dimension Tables:

  • Dim Product: Includes product details like price, category, and name.
  • Dim Customer: Stores customer demographics such as age, city, and gender.
  • Calendar: A date dimension table for time-based analysis.

Data Cleaning and SQL Code

Data cleaning and preprocessing are vital steps in ensuring the quality and accuracy of analysis. A range of SQL queries were used for data cleaning and preprocessing. Using SQL, I streamlined data workflows by crafting queries to categorize products based on pricing tiers, enrich customer data with geographic insights by joining dim_customers and dim_geography, and identify and tag duplicate records with Common Table Expressions (CTEs). Additionally, I addressed whitespace issues in review text for better readability and cleaned the engagement_data table to normalize values for advanced analytics. These SQL solutions exemplify my ability to handle complex data challenges efficiently and prepare datasets for actionable insights. Below is a featured example: 

            -- Outer query selects the final cleaned and standardized data
    
            SELECT 
                JourneyID,  -- Selects the unique identifier for each journey to ensure data traceability
                CustomerID,  -- Selects the unique identifier for each customer to link journeys to specific customers
                ProductID,  -- Selects the unique identifier for each product to analyze customer interactions with different products
                VisitDate,  -- Selects the date of the visit to understand the timeline of customer interactions
                Stage,  -- Uses the uppercased stage value from the subquery for consistency in analysis
                Action,  -- Selects the action taken by the customer (e.g., View, Click, Purchase)
                COALESCE(Duration, avg_duration) AS Duration  -- Replaces missing durations with the average duration for the corresponding date
            FROM 
                (
                    -- Subquery to process and clean the data
                    SELECT 
                        JourneyID,  -- Selects the unique identifier for each journey to ensure data traceability
                        CustomerID,  -- Selects the unique identifier for each customer to link journeys to specific customers
                        ProductID,  -- Selects the unique identifier for each product to analyze customer interactions with different products
                        VisitDate,  -- Selects the date of the visit to understand the timeline of customer interactions
                        UPPER(Stage) AS Stage,  -- Converts Stage values to uppercase for consistency in data analysis
                        Action,  -- Selects the action taken by the customer (e.g., View, Click, Purchase)
                        Duration,  -- Uses Duration directly, assuming it's already a numeric type
                        AVG(Duration) OVER (PARTITION BY VisitDate) AS avg_duration,  -- Calculates the average duration for each date, using only numeric values
                        ROW_NUMBER() OVER (
                            PARTITION BY CustomerID, ProductID, VisitDate, UPPER(Stage), Action  -- Groups by these columns to identify duplicate records
                            ORDER BY JourneyID  -- Orders by JourneyID to keep the first occurrence of each duplicate
                        ) AS row_num  -- Assigns a row number to each row within the partition to identify duplicates
                    FROM 
                        dbo.customer_journey  -- Specifies the source table from which to select the data
                ) AS subquery  -- Names the subquery for reference in the outer query
            WHERE 
                row_num = 1;  -- Keeps only the first occurrence of each duplicate group identified in the subquery

Python Code for Sentiment Analysis

This sentiment analysis workflow utilizes Python to process customer reviews extracted from a SQL Server database. After establishing a secure connection to the database, the review data is fetched into a pandas DataFrame using the pyodbc library. The VADER (Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner) sentiment analyzer from the NLTK library is then used to analyze the sentiment of the review text.
The analysis involves several steps:
Sentiment Scoring:
Each review's text is processed using VADER to compute a compound sentiment score ranging from -1 (most negative) to 1 (most positive).
Sentiment Categorization:
The sentiment scores are combined with the review ratings to classify each review into meaningful categories such as Positive, Negative, Neutral, or Mixed Positive/Negative, depending on the interplay of text sentiment and numeric ratings.
Sentiment Bucketing:
The compound scores are further grouped into predefined ranges (e.g., -1.0 to -0.5 for strongly negative, 0.5 to 1.0 for strongly positive), providing a structured view of sentiment intensity.
The resulting dataset, enriched with sentiment scores, categories, and buckets, is saved as a CSV file for further analysis and visualization. This comprehensive approach not only evaluates textual sentiment but also contextualizes it with customer-provided ratings, ensuring a robust understanding of customer feedback. 

            # Define a function to categorize sentiment using both the sentiment score and the review rating
            def categorize_sentiment(score, rating):
                # Use both the text sentiment score and the numerical rating to determine sentiment category
                if score > 0.05:  # Positive sentiment score
                    if rating >= 4:
                        return 'Positive'  # High rating and positive sentiment
                    elif rating == 3:
                        return 'Mixed Positive'  # Neutral rating but positive sentiment
                    else:
                        return 'Mixed Negative'  # Low rating but positive sentiment
                elif score < -0.05:  # Negative sentiment score
                    if rating <= 2:
                        return 'Negative'  # Low rating and negative sentiment
                    elif rating == 3:
                        return 'Mixed Negative'  # Neutral rating but negative sentiment
                    else:
                        return 'Mixed Positive'  # High rating but negative sentiment
                else:  # Neutral sentiment score
                    if rating >= 4:
                        return 'Positive'  # High rating with neutral sentiment
                    elif rating <= 2:
                        return 'Negative'  # Low rating with neutral sentiment
                    else:
                        return 'Neutral'  # Neutral rating and neutral sentiment
            
            # Define a function to bucket sentiment scores into text ranges
            def sentiment_bucket(score):
                if score >= 0.5:
                    return '0.5 to 1.0'  # Strongly positive sentiment
                elif 0.0 <= score < 0.5:
                    return '0.0 to 0.49'  # Mildly positive sentiment
                elif -0.5 <= score < 0.0:
                    return '-0.49 to 0.0'  # Mildly negative sentiment
                else:
                    return '-1.0 to -0.5'  # Strongly negative sentiment

Overview Analysis

This section provides an in-depth analysis of key metrics such as conversion rates, customer engagement, and feedback, along with actionable recommendations for improvement.

Overview

  • Decreased Conversion Rates: The conversion rate demonstrated a strong rebound in December, reaching 10.3%, despite a notable dip to 5.0% in October.
  • Reduced Customer Engagement: There is a decline in overall social media engagement, with views dropping throughout the year. While clicks and likes are low compared to views, the click-through rate stands at 15.37%, meaning that engaged users are still interacting effectively.
  • Customer Feedback Analysis: Customer ratings have remained consistent, averaging around 3.7 throughout the year. Although stable, the average rating is below the target of 4.0, suggesting a need for focused improvements in customer satisfaction, for products below 3,5.

Decreased Conversion Rates

  • General Conversion Trend: Conversion rates varied throughout the year, with February and July performing strongly due to seasonal peaks.
  • Lowest Conversion Month: May had the lowest conversion rate (4.48%), indicating a need for revisiting marketing strategies.
  • Highest Conversion Rates: January led with an 19.61% conversion rate, largely driven by seasonal demand for Ski Boots (150% conversion).

Reduced Customer Engagement

  • Declining Views: Views peaked in February and July but declined steadily from August onward.
  • Low Interaction Rates: Clicks and likes were low compared to views, highlighting the need for more engaging content.
  • Content Type Performance: Blog content performed best, especially in April and July, while social media and videos showed steady but lower engagement.

Customer Feedback Analysis

  • Customer Ratings Distribution: Most ratings are positive (4-5 stars), but a notable proportion of lower ratings suggests areas for improvement.
  • Sentiment Analysis: Positive sentiment dominates (275 reviews), with 82 reviews showing negative sentiment.
  • Opportunity for Improvement: Addressing mixed and negative reviews could boost satisfaction and improve the average rating closer to the 4.0 target.

Actions

  • Increase Conversion Rates: Focus marketing on high-conversion products like Ski Boots and Kayaks. Utilize seasonal promotions and personalized campaigns during peak months.
  • Enhance Customer Engagement: Experiment with engaging formats like interactive videos and user-generated content. Optimize calls-to-action for better engagement, especially during low-performing months.
  • Improve Customer Feedback Scores: Analyze mixed and negative reviews to identify and address common concerns. Follow up with dissatisfied customers to resolve issues and encourage re-rating.
